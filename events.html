Background: Once upon a time gone, there was a royal family who prided themselves on their ability to bond with Pokemon. Strong warriors sparring with their powerful companions, fair maidens escorting their dear friends to and fro, and travelers feeling safe knowing that battle or ambush can be fought with their partner by their side. This family sat well and lavish within a forgotten kingdom, destined for a rich bloodline.
Until the Great War between Truth and Ideals broke out.
Whispers of who followed what reached the ears of the kingdom and the seeds of disarray sprouted like wildfire as citizens slowly grew distant from each other. Eventually, even those of the royal family fell victim to the age-old question: what value matters more in life - finding the truth and living by it, or following your ideals to the end and spreading them far and wide? Some went against this ultimatum, notably the daughter of the royal house's lord and lady, Eleanor. She saw that a world can live with both and to think one better than the other is foolhardy to seek conflict over.
Others did not agree.
Without mercy, she was cast out from her family and sent to the wolves like pigs to slaughter. They captured her, tortured her, and made her cry for nights on end as she pined for her family to forgive her and take her back in. There was no end to the creativity that rage-stricken peasants could use to make her life hell on earth, and every day was more and more reason to not go on. But she stayed strong, knowing that her truth to allow ideals to flourish while knowing the facts behind them will keep her alive through such a dark time.
But her will cannot stop the sharp end of a blade.
With one last cry out for her family that had forsaken her, Eleanor pleaded for the war to end and to bring peace to her kingdom and her family, having her final words cut short as she was stabbed clean through her broken heart and left to choke on her waning life before succumbing. The echoes of her cry did not go unnoticed, for the royal family - albeit still bitter for her not following their ideologies - were enraged at the news of her death.
Soon, the war fell into the lines of the innocent. Men, women, children, old and young, friends and lovers, all giving up their bonds for the sake to settle a score. The bloodshed was violent, and lasted for many an hour before finally, a cry was heard.
The cry of the slain princess, born again as a warrior of faith.
Competitive niche? (you are also allowed to provide sample sets)
Eledaia is a wall breaker, built to cover a wide selection of various walls thanks to its myriad of attacks favoring its notable Special Attack. Calm Mind sets allow it to build itself into either a strong late-game sweeper with STAB and coverage for Steel(Flamethrower/Fire Blast/Earth Power), Poison or strong Fighting types(Stored Power), or Bug types that resist Fairy. Nasty Plot sets lets it utilize its Special Attack much sooner at the cost of no extra bulk to ward off specially attacking walls that can sit and whittle it down. It also has a niche in being a pivot with Intimidate for an ability and Parting Shot to get back out before it gets hurt. Hydreigon, Iron Hands, Dragapult, and other OU threats have a hard time sitting in on Eledaia due to its decent STAB combo.
Leftover sets appreciate compiling passive healing with Recover, which allows it to live longer in a game of hazard chip that cannot be cleared.
Eledaia does suffer from being checked by common Scarfs like Gengar, offensive sets being walled by Toxapex or Unaware, defensive sets having to wait for things like Gholdengo or Crucibelle to be knocked out, or being forced back out from being able to set up by Venomicon or Cawmodore.
Eledaia-Reborn is a wallbreaker built to cut through most walls. Swords Dance or even Life Orb sets make use of its vast array of physical moves, and Sharpness boosts all Slicing attacks to make normal checks risky. Although, Choice Band is a niche set that lets it forgo setup to use raw power instead, letting it fill in one more attack. Double Dance sets allow it to make use of Power Trip not being immune to anything compared to base Eledaia's Stored Power, and Weakness Policy sets have grown to a noticeable degree thanks to its decent 80/120/100 bulk to survive at least two non-Fighting attacks.
Defensive sets lose out on the ability to lower attack stats twice with Battle Armor instead of Intimidate, and supportive sets are lacking when its only moves to help a teammate in Double Battles are stat-lowering ones. Eledaia-Reborn's typing makes it a glass cannon to the numerous Fighting types in the meta, and defensive walls that can switch in on it carry Body Press, which limit the amount of opportunities it has to come in and clean up. Corviknight can live a Fire Last and hit back with Body Press, while things like Iron Hands or Cinderace shut it down before it can get going.
Eledaia-Blade appreciates teammates like Mollux who can help it stay out of fighting Toxapex, Plasmanta for Kerfluffle switchins, or Amoonguss to stop Breloom from clicking Mach Punch on it.
